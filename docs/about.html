About

Instrumental music, composed while it plays — right here in your browser.

No recordings, no samples, no server. Pick a style, press play. Every piece is reproducible: the seed and your settings live in the page address, so copying the link keeps the piece exactly.

How it works

This site is a single self-contained folder of original code. It composes each piece from scratch — harmony, melody, rhythm, form — and performs it with expressive timing through the Web Audio API, fetching nothing from the network.

At Intermediate and Advanced levels the site can also invent a style — a seeded new point in style space with its own audible signatures. Every setting can change while the music plays: the piece morphs at the next musical boundary instead of restarting, because the composer only ever writes a few seconds ahead of what you hear.

On iPhone and iPad, playback pauses when the screen locks or you leave Safari — iOS keeps only recorded media playing in the background, and this music is generated live.

Using the controls

The controls come in three tiers, revealed by the Start / Intermediate / Advanced toggle at the top. Start has a handful of headline dials; Intermediate adds about twenty familiar controls; Advanced opens the full set, including invented parameters. Whatever you set carries over as you move between tiers.

Every control starts on auto — the seed decides its value, so a dropdown reads “choose…” and a slider shows no number until you touch it. Moving a control pins it to your choice (its label turns amber); the auto chip beside it — or picking “choose…” again in a dropdown — un-pins it back to auto. Changes land while the music plays: the piece morphs at the next musical boundary instead of restarting.

New piece rolls a fresh seed for everything you haven’t pinned, within the current style. Continuous keeps going — when a piece ends, another begins in the same style. Reset returns every setting to auto. Copy link copies a URL that reproduces the exact piece; copy seed copies just the seed. Background (experimental, for phones and tablets) renders the current piece to an audio file so it keeps playing when the screen locks.

Choosing the instruments

At the Advanced tier the palette dropdown is replaced by a grid of instrument checkboxes — the exact instruments in the piece. Check one to add it, uncheck to remove it.

How many instruments a piece has, and which, is decided when the piece is composed — it is not fixed in advance. From the style, the energy and density, and the seed, the composer draws an ensemble of musical roles — a lead line, chords, a bass, and perhaps drums, a pad, or a drone — and gives each role an instrument; quieter, sparser settings use fewer, and the arrangement can add or drop voices as the music’s intensity rises and falls through the piece. Because the checkboxes act on that composed ensemble, unchecking an instrument reliably removes it, but checking one adds sound only if the piece has a role for it to play — adding a horn won’t be heard if nothing in the current arrangement calls for one.

The seed

Every piece grows from a seed — a single number shown in the seed box, which you can edit. The seed together with your pinned settings completely determines the music: the same seed and settings always produce the same piece, note for note, and a different seed gives a different piece in the same style. Both are stored in the page address, so copying the link (or just the seed) lets you — or anyone you send it to — return to exactly this piece later. That is what makes every piece here reproducible rather than a one-time stream.

Who makes it

Everything here is written and built by Claude (Anthropic’s AI), across many autonomous sessions directed by Tom Gally, who judges the music. The design documents describe how it all works. The code, the music, and the prose are dedicated to the public domain.
