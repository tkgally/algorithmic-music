algorithmic-music

An experiment in making genuinely good instrumental music with code in the browser — plain HTML, CSS, and JavaScript with the Web Audio API. No samples, no libraries, no build step, no server: every note is synthesized live, and each engine runs entirely offline from a single folder.

The project is a long-running collaboration between Tom Gally, who directs it and judges the music, and Claude (Anthropic's AI), which does all of the research, writing, and engineering across autonomous sessions. Its bet: browser music generators are easy to build but usually musically disappointing, and the cure is knowledge plus iteration — a serious, cited knowledge base about how music works and why people enjoy it, driving engine designs that improve through evaluation and listener feedback. Everything here — this page, the engines, and the knowledge base — is written and built by Claude, and dedicated to the public domain (CC0).

The engines

Each engine is a self-contained, deliberately different generative instrument, newest first. Pick one and press play; every piece is reproducible from a seed and shareable by link. Open a card for the details.

ENGINE 04 Cantabile a small ensemble that sings every note Play →
About this engine

The expressive engine. Four original synthetic instruments — aria (warm, bowed), reed (breathy), wire (electric, gritty), and glass (pure) — that can scoop into a note, bend it, make it swell, and let a vibrato bloom, the way a violinist, a saxophonist, or a lead guitarist shapes a single sustained tone. Each piece is a short modal arc — theme, a call-and-response dialogue, a rise to a climax, a transformed return, and a real ending — and the players intensify toward the moments that matter. Which voices lead and answer varies with the seed and your choice. The sliders are the expression itself: Song (how vocal the pitch is), Bloom (how a held note grows), Ardor (how dramatic the arc), and a master Expression.

ENGINE 03 Groove / Lo-Fi a looped lo-fi beat — swing, backbeat, jazzy chords Play →
About this engine

The trio's groove engine: a looped lo-fi hip-hop beat built to make you nod. A backbeat snare that drags behind the pulse, a warm swung kick at medium syncopation, ghost notes and a sparse bell in the second half, jazzy 7th/9th electric-piano chords, a weighty bass — and a little vinyl crackle over the top. Groove comes from structured pattern, swing, and a velocity hierarchy, not timing noise. Choose the mood (major 7ths, minor 9ths, dorian, tape), tempo, swing, and space.

ENGINE 02 Ambient Drift weightless, beatless, slow — Eno's Airports trick Play →
About this engine

The deliberate opposite of a beat: weightless, beatless, and slow. Sustained tones and sparse bells run on periods that never quite line up — Brian Eno's Music for Airports trick — over a drone that drifts through a single mode. It emerges from silence and dissolves back into it. As ignorable as it is interesting; choose the mood (lydian, pentatonic, dorian, minor), the pace, and the space.

ENGINE 01 Tonal Classical a short classical piece built to actually finish Play →
About this engine

A short piece in classical style, built to actually finish: a theme (a parallel period that asks and answers with real cadences), a contrasting middle, a varied return of the theme at the climax, and a closing coda that accelerates into a final cadence and a lengthened last chord. Harmony is generated backward from its cadences; melody is shaped contour-first around pinned goal tones. Major or minor, any key, adjustable tempo and reverb.

More engines are planned — deliberately contrasting genres and architectures (non-Western tuning systems among them), each built from the same knowledge base and numbered so they are easy to reference and compare.

How it works

  • Knowledge base. Claude researches music theory, the psychology of listening, genre traditions, composition craft, algorithmic techniques, Web Audio implementation, and evaluation — every page ending in concrete implications for engines, every claim cited.
  • Engines. Contrasting generators built from that knowledge, published here on GitHub Pages. Each is fully transparent about being AI-designed, deterministic from a seed, and downloadable to run offline.
  • Improvement loop. Listener feedback and computational metrics feed findings back into the knowledge base, and the engines get better over time.
