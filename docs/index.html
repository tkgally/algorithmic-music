algorithmic-music

A single, self-contained website that composes and performs instrumental music in the browser — across a wide range of styles: some drawn from familiar musical traditions, and some invented on the fly by the site's own code. Every note is synthesized live with the Web Audio API. No samples, no outside libraries, no server.

This is a long-running collaboration between Tom Gally, who directs the project and judges the music, and Claude (Anthropic's AI), which does all of the research, writing, and engineering across many autonomous sessions. The bet: browser music generators are easy to build but usually musically disappointing, and the cure is knowledge plus iteration — a serious, cited knowledge base about how music works and why people enjoy it, driving designs that improve through evaluation and listener feedback. Everything here — this site, its engines, and the knowledge base — is written and built by Claude, and dedicated to the public domain (CC0).

Where this is going

The comprehensive site now taking shape will offer three levels of control, so it suits a first-time visitor and a curious tinkerer alike:

  • Start — a few familiar genre choices; pick one, press play, and a random seed does the rest.
  • Intermediate — around twenty more detailed but still familiar controls.
  • Advanced — a wide range of controls, including parameters invented specifically for this site.

Alongside the familiar styles, the site will compose in original styles created on the fly — built from high-order principles about structure, harmony, rhythm, melody, and timbre, inspired by existing genres while adding elements of their own. Every piece stays fully reproducible from a compact, shareable link, so a piece you like can always be found again. Playlists and other conveniences will come later.

How it works

  • Knowledge base. Claude researches music theory, the psychology of listening, genre traditions, composition craft, algorithmic techniques, Web Audio implementation, and evaluation — every page ending in concrete implications for engines, every claim cited.
  • Engines & site. Generators built from that knowledge, published here on GitHub Pages. Everything is transparent about being AI-designed, deterministic from a seed, and runnable offline from a single folder.
  • Improvement loop. Listener feedback and computational metrics feed findings back into the knowledge base, and the music gets better over time.
