## The static: diagnosed empirically before touching code

Because the static was reported on **both** engines and had survived v0.2's offline-validated reverb fix, I built spectral/analysis probes (headless Chromium + OfflineAudioContext, in scratchpad) rather than guess:

- Per-voice isolated renders (dry vs through the fx chain): **no** HF hiss (energy > 7 kHz ≈ 0), **no** aliasing, **no** chain-added distortion (a sat/comp/reverb A/B was flat).
- Whole-engine renders: an 8–18 kHz burst detector and a click/outlier detector found nothing real — the largest sample steps (~0.1) are the naturally steep segments of legitimate FM/bell waveforms, one waveform-period apart.
- Canvas spectrograms (rendered to PNG and inspected): the audio is **clean**. The FM `keys` vibrato's "beading" is the ordinary STFT signature of *frequency* vibrato (confirmed: modulating carrier+modulator together, preserving the FM ratio, changed nothing), **not** audible AM.
- The one thing that *is* visible and measurable: **reverb-tail grain**. A sustained tone through the reverb alone has a fluctuating wet envelope; its short-time-energy coefficient of variation was **cv ≈ 0.49** for the shipped white-noise IR. Since Engine 02's voices are pure sines/band-limited triangles (can't alias), the shared reverb was the only possible source of its grain — consistent with both reports.

**Conclusion:** the offline render is clean apart from reverb grain; any *remaining* static is a real-time (CPU/GC-underrun) phenomenon the offline gate cannot reproduce — which is exactly why v0.2's offline-validated fix didn't land for the listener.

## Fixes

**Shared `fx.js` (both engines) — smoother reverb, not darker.** `makeIR`'s noise source is now **near-Gaussian** (mean of three uniforms, an Irwin-Hall variate) instead of flat white: uniform noise sits near its extremes more often, giving a rougher decaying-tail envelope; a Gaussian source has fewer outliers and a smoother one. Grain **cv 0.49 → 0.33 (−31%)**, energy-normalized to a fixed target RMS so reverb loudness is unchanged. A split probe proved this is *not* re-darkening (v0.2 already darkened and it didn't work): adding a *third* lowpass pole *raised* grain to 0.51, while the Gaussian source at the original two poles produced the drop. Re-vendored into all three engines.

**All three `index.html` — `latencyHint: 'playback'`** on the live `AudioContext` (larger buffers), the documented cure for real-time crackle/underruns (`web-audio-fundamentals.md`, `audio-worklets-and-performance.md`). Offline gates don't exercise it; the new UI-smoke gate at least loads it.

**Engine 01 melody variety → v0.3.0 (`composer.js`).** The launch composer hard-coded one shape: a straight rise to an apex fixed at the last basic-idea slot. `composePiece` now picks a **contour archetype per piece** — arch / arch-late / descending (opens *on* the peak, falls) / ascending (the old shape) / wave — weighted toward Huron's two dominant types, with a **contrasting** archetype for the B section, and a per-archetype start-register offset so descending shapes start high. Across 200 seeds the opening gesture is now ~50% up / ~24% flat / ~26% down and the apex lands early/mid/late. Contour is only a target envelope; `fillContour`'s constraints (chord tones on strong beats, step bias, reversal, apex uniqueness) are untouched, so lines stay idiomatic. `composePeriod` (the tested reference path, 15 tests) is left unchanged — variety lives entirely in the whole-piece path.

**Engine 03 comp → v0.2.0 (`composer.js`).** Three faults behind "every seed the same / overpowers the drone":
- Register jumping — v0.1 voiced each chord independently into a two-octave window. Now **voice-led** (hold common tones, move only new tones to the octave nearest a per-piece center): consecutive-chord centroid motion dropped to **≈ 1.3 semitones** → a stable bed, not a melody.
- Sameness — the progression was mood-fixed. Each mood now has a **bank of four progressions** picked per seed, plus a per-seed **comping rhythm** (mostly sustained/legato) and voicing center. Seeds now differ in harmony + texture.
- The missing "drone" — a soft, dark **sustained root+fifth pad** now rings under the comp every bar (shared `pad` voice), and the rhodes comp is softer and legato so it supports rather than leads.
