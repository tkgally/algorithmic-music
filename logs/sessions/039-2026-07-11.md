# Session 039 — 2026-07-11 — Expression layer · richer voices · invented repeated-note fix · Jazz withdrawn

**Scope.** Tom's third feedback pass, four requests: (1) a greater variety of instrumental voices — the melodic voices read as "plonky" (short, fast-fading, fixed-pitch, near-staccato); he wants richer, more complex tones, some with variable frequency (vibrato-scale); (2) more expressive, human-like performance — legato overlap in melodies, more rubato — with his suggestion to treat expression as a separate post-composition step informed by each passage's role in the whole piece, even if that meant abandoning compose-on-the-fly (he left the architecture decision to this session); (3) more varied invented styles, and an end to pieces that are mainly one repeated note (he linked `#p=CwGngWyPIAAAAAA`); (4) remove Jazz from the UI and record his reaction in the wiki.

## The architecture decision (request 2) — the JIT loop stays

Tom asked whether expression needs whole-piece pre-composition. It does not, because of a property the site already has: **the piece's dramatic form — sections, their kinds, intensities, the arc, total bars — is fully decided in `strategy.init()` before a single note sounds.** Only note-level realization is just-in-time. So a post-composition expression pass can know each passage's role in the entire piece under JIT: the composer wrapper now stamps every unit with `tPiece`/`arcLevel`/`sectionIdx` (one central edit in `compose.js create()` — no pack changes), notes already carry structural weight and tags (apex, cadence, phraseStart, breath, ending), and the expression layer reads all of it. Whole-piece-first would have added only note-level lookahead across future phrases (marginal for tone/volume/timing contours) while forfeiting live no-restart changes, the two-bar crossfade, and continuous play. Bonus over Tom's expectation: **expression parameters remain live-changeable** — nothing had to become restart-only.

## What shipped

### 1. The expression layer (`docs/lib/perform.js`, substantially rewritten)

A documented, deterministic pass sequence (header comment explains the whole design):

- **Phrase analysis** (`analyzePhrases`, exported): each melodic line (lead/counter voices, non-percussive) is segmented into slur groups at composed rests (> 0.26 beats), `phraseStart`/`breath` tags, and after cadence/ending notes; each group learns its apex and every note its position, slur-to-next, slur-from-prev.
- **Legato** (Tom's "successive notes that overlap slightly, like a piano melody played legato"): inside a slur group, successive *different-pitch* notes overlap by `min(0.14, 0.035 + 0.22·slot)·legato` seconds; repeated pitches re-articulate (0.72); phrase-final notes breathe (0.9); cadence/ending notes ring slightly past full value; the apex lingers. The **legato amount follows the style**: `0.25 + 0.55·expression + 0.3·rubato − 0.45·swing − 0.18·(timeline present)` — classical ≈ 0.84, lofi ≈ 0.3 (below the 0.35 gate → stays détaché). Tested both directions.
- **Rubato v2**: phrase-arch depth raised to `0.16·expression·rubato·(1.15 − 0.5·intensity)` capped 0.09 (~±8–9% tempo inside a phrase for classical; grooves stay tight) — quiet passages breathe more than peaks. **Agogics**: a slight hesitation entering a phrase (never on the unit downbeat), a lean on the apex.
- **Per-phrase dynamics**: melodic lines get a crescendo into their own phrase apex and easing after (replacing the flat per-unit arch there); phrase ends soften unless cadential.
- **Portamento** (Tom's "variable frequencies"): a slurred step (≤ 7 semitones) on a sustained expressive voice glides from the previous pitch (~90% of the interval over 0.04–0.11 s) — frequency now varies *between* notes, not only within them.
- **Structural drive**: intra-note expression (vibrato depth/rate, swell, brightness, grain) now scales with `expression × weight × (0.4 + 0.8·arcLevel)` — the arc level folds the section's place in the whole form into every note.
- **Latent gaps found while wiring** (all silently inert since session 034): the performer emitted `scoop` but voices consume `onsetCents` (scoops never sounded — now translated); `swellPos` should have been `swellPeak`; `drift` was never set, so the Engine-04 microDrift "alive pitch" cue was OFF in the site — now on for all expressive voices.

### 2. Three new rich sustained voices (`docs/lib/synth.js`) + wiring (request 1)

All original, imitating no specific instrument, all reading `note.expr` (vibrato/swell/scoop/portamento/drift/grain), all built on the proven organic-timbre helpers (`exprEnv`/`pitchExpr`/`microDrift`/`bodyResonance`/`onsetTransient`/`breathLayer`):

- **`organ`** — a glowing additive stack (two slightly-detuned ranks that beat gently) with a delayed tremulant; the steadiest voice.
- **`horn`** — dark blooming brass-like lead: two detuned saws + a sub-octave through a lowpass that *opens through the note* (the brass bloom), fixed tube-body formants, breath-chiff onset.
- **`voce`** — a singing vowel voice: saw+triangle through two resonant formant paths that morph during the note (a mouth is never still) over a dark fundamental path, breath grain, deeper default vibrato; the strongest variable-pitch carrier. The formant paths are lossy, so it carries an explicit makeup gain (isolated-note renders level-matched all three to ≈ −30 dBFS).

Wiring so they are actually heard: two new generic palettes on every style ("Voice & organ", "Dark brass" — `EXTRA_PALETTES` 5→7); one new *authored* palette each for classical (Horn & strings), ambient (Voices — choral drift), cinematic (Brass swell), folk (Singer), so auto seeds sometimes pick them; 8 new invented ensemble templates (32→40); `MASTER_INSTRUMENTS` 24→27 (append-only) so Advanced checkboxes can add them. **URL: V3 layout** in `serialize.js` — identical to V2 except the instrument mask widened 24→27 bits; encode writes V3, decode reads V1/V2/V3 (old links unaffected; round-trip + back-compat tested; `_selftest` assertion updated).

### 3. The invented repeated-note fix + variety (request 3)

Tom's link decodes to seed `c069e05b` — measured: the lead played one MIDI pitch **61 times consecutively** (3 distinct pitches in 65 notes). Diagnosis found **three interlocking causes** (full detail in `wiki/findings-invented-composer.md`):

1. **The gamut bug (the real headline):** `theory.SCALES` entries are semitone *offsets*; session 038's `degreePcs`/`triadPcs` (invent.js) and the interval-cell matcher (style.js) accumulated them as *step sizes*, so the "melodic gamut" was mostly out-of-scale pitch classes. The ≥ 4-note pool guards usually made this degrade silently (gamut inert — full-scale melody); but when the bogus set's accidental overlap with the true scale spanned only 1–2 pitch classes with ≥ 4 pool notes, the melodic pool collapsed to those notes (a hocket seed hammered one pitch 68×). The tintinnabuli T-voice was also shadowing at wrong intervals. **The 038 "identity gamut" feature only now does what it was designed to do.**
2. **Chorale over drone:** the chorale top voice hard-filtered to chord tones; a drone never changes chords → one reachable pitch. Fixed twice over: coupling (`chorale + drone → modal`) and the top voice rewritten as a real melodic chooser (per-phrase arch contour, chord membership a scored bonus *never* a filter, explicit repeat penalty, cadence still lands the tonic).
3. **Strata parking:** the skeleton's converging walk sat on its goal once reached; now it swings to a pool neighbor after two repeats (upper/lower auxiliary, gamelan-style) and the figuration avoids re-picking its previous note.

**Standing guards:** `site-check` now fails any non-percussion piece whose melodic lead has a same-pitch run > 16 or < 4 distinct pitches (≥ 24 notes) — every run and sweep, presets included; node tests pin Tom's exact seed (distinct ≥ 5, run ≤ 8) plus a 24-seed invented sweep (run ≤ 12, distinct ≥ 4) plus gamut-⊆-scale across 25 seeds plus the chorale-never-drone coupling. Post-fix worst case over 60 seeds: run 9 (an ostinato-like figure); Tom's seed: 12 distinct pitches, max run 2, and (via the texture coupling below) a `voce` lead.

**Variety:** drone weight trimmed in the invented harmony draw (0.18 → 0.14); the 8 new sustained ensembles; chorale/tintinnabuli usually remap a struck lead to a sustained voice (70%, seeded — held-tone textures need held tones). Invented seeds redraw (third session running); presets/melds keep their notes (their performances gain the expression layer).
