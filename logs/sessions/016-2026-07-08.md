### Built `experiments/lib/theory.js`

Same dual-format (UMD-lite) shape as `rng.js`/`transport.js`. Scope, deliberately bounded to what the wiki pages above actually specify numbers for:

- Note names ↔ MIDI (scientific pitch notation, C4=60) ↔ frequency (A440); pitch classes.
- Size-only interval naming (documented limitation: cannot distinguish A4 from d5 — that needs letter-based spelling, out of scope) and transposition.
- 13 named 12-TET scales as semitone patterns — deliberately **excluding** the "yo" scale, because `tuning-and-scales.md` itself flags that scale's degree spelling as unverified (its cited source names it only as a contrast without spelling out degrees); including an unverified specific would violate `conventions.md`'s "don't state folklore as fact" rule.
- Krumhansl & Kessler (1982) tonal-hierarchy profiles, transcribed verbatim, via a `stability(pc, tonicPc, mode)` accessor.
- Generic tertian chord construction: `triad`/`seventh`/`diatonicChords` count diatonic thirds and classify the resulting semitone intervals, so quality and roman numerals fall out of *any* 7-note scale (this is what correctly produces harmonic minor's augmented III without a hand-written per-scale table).
- T/S/D functional-harmony labels, but **only** for major and natural-minor scales (where the theory is settled and cross-checks against Tonal's own convention) — modal scales return `function: null` rather than a guessed label.
- Piston's root-progression table and the rock-corpus chord/pre-tonic priors, transcribed from `harmony.md` and pre-weighted 0.6/0.3/0.1 by tier exactly as that page recommends, via `pistonSuccessors(roman)` returning `{items, weights}` shaped for a `rng.weighted()`-style picker.
- Cents-based tuning tables (slendro, a rast-like maqam approximation, the *in* scale in cents, JI major-scale ratios) plus `freqFromCents`/`freqFromRatio`, transcribed from `tuning-and-scales.md`'s "concrete tables to ship" list — these operate on cents/ratios, not the semitone/MIDI machinery above, since they are not 12-TET.

### Tested and validated

Wrote 26 tests in `experiments/tests/theory.test.js` (`node experiments/tests/run.js` → **51 passing, 0 failed**, up from 25). Then ran a much wider one-off cross-check against Tonal that is not itself committed (Tonal isn't a repo dependency) but whose *results* are recorded in the findings page:

- All 128 MIDI notes (0-127) round-tripped through `midiToNoteName` and back through Tonal's `Note.midi` — exact match.
- Every one of the 13 `SCALES` entries compared against Tonal's `Scale.get(...).notes` (converted to pitch classes) across **all 12 tonic pitch classes** (156 scale instances) — exact match.
- `diatonicChords` for both `major` and `naturalMinor` compared (as sorted pitch-class sets, so spelling-independent) against Tonal's `Key.majorKey`/`Key.minorKey(...).natural` triads across **all 12 tonics** (24 key instances) — exact match.

Zero mismatches across all of the above. This is a stronger validation than the hand-picked test-file assertions alone, and is now the standing practice recorded in `shared-libraries.md` for future pure modules: sweep wide against an oracle at dev time in a scratch directory, then bake the facts that matter into permanent, dependency-free, committed tests.
