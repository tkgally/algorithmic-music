## The engine — `percussion@0.1.0`

Built at `docs/engines/05-percussion/`, cataloged in the hub as "Percussion Ensemble." Follows the engine-architecture pipeline (composer → performer → synth → scheduler).

### Voices (additive to `synth.js`) — three generators, seven original voices

Original, sample-free, imitating no named instrument. Three reusable generators: **pitch-drop membrane**, **modal bank** (a `modalBank` helper summing decaying inharmonic sines), and **shaped noise** (a `noiseBurst` helper). The seven voices: `boom` (low anchor drum), `drum` (mid hand-drum with bass/tone/slap/mute tags + inharmonic membrane partials), `wood` (block/clave, free-bar ratios), `metal` (cowbell/agogô/bell, clangy inharmonic bank keeping a ~1.2 tierce), `gong` (plate/crash — dense cluster + noise cloud with a nonlinear attack **bloom** + long tail), `shaker` (two-phase filtered-noise burst), `mallet` (tuned marimba/xylophone bar — the pitched melodic-accompaniment voice). Each reads an optional `note.p` params object (**tune / decay / attack / bright / noise / inharm**) and `note.pan`.

### Composer (`experiments/composers/percussion.js`) — three worlds, meter-parametric

- **Three styles.** `ensemble` (concert): states a **rhythmic cell** and develops it across the ensemble (rotation/displacement/augmentation/diminution/thinning) over a rising **density arc**, with unison hits, a break, a metallic climax, and a **real gong ending**. `folk`: an inviolable **timeline** (son/rumba clave on 4/4, the 12-pulse standard bell on 12/8, Euclidean on odd meters) + interlocking Euclidean support ostinati + a low anchor + an **improvising lead** + calls/breaks; cyclic, layers enter one at a time and shed at the end. `circle`: a shared **heartbeat**, parts join loosely, a **rumble** swells, a **stop-cut**, call-and-echo, a **fade**.
- **Fully meter-parametric.** A bar = eighth-pulse beat-groups over a sixteenth grid, emitted in quarter-note beats, so additive meters are unequal groups over a constant pulse. Nine meters ship (defaults 2/4, 4/4; 3/4, 6/8, 12/8, aksak 5/8/7/8, 5/4, 7/4); bars snap toward the 4/8/16-bar phrase group.
- **Optional melodic accompaniment.** A quiet mallet ostinato (mode per style) + a soft bass pulse, locked to the timeline, kept under the drums; toggles off at `melody = 0`.

### Performer (`experiments/engines/percussion/engine.js`) — structured feel + macros + accel/rit

Pure `renderPlan`. Fixed per-voice laid-back offsets (anchor tight, metals/shakers back), a style-scaled AR(1) residual (circle ≈ 1.6× looser than concert), swing on duple feels only, and **per-section tempo shaping** (concert ritardando ending, circle rumble accelerando; taiko oroshi in spirit) as a per-bar local tempo so the planner stays deterministic. The four **timbre macros** (Tune/Ring/Attack/Brightness) fold into every note's `p` — the "adjustable timbre, attack, fade."

### Mix (additive to `fx.js`)

Four percussion buses: `percLow` (full-range, near-dry — the anchor), `percMid`/`percHi` (high-passed, light space), `malletBus` (chord-like), plus in-voice stereo seating.

## Key decisions

- **Additive, engine-05-local, zero cross-engine risk.** All `synth.js`/`fx.js` changes are new voices/helpers/buses; the canonical libs were re-vendored **only** into `docs/engines/05-percussion/`. Engines 01–04 keep their own older vendored copies → byte-for-byte unaffected (138 canonical-lib tests pass; ui-smoke plays all five with 0 errors).
- **Voice *generators*, not a fixed kit.** Three functions (membrane / modal / noise) + a six-macro surface produce the whole palette and make new colors a one-line preset — reusable by future engines.
- **Density/intensity arc as the form.** With no harmony, a per-section intensity target driving layer count / onset density / dynamics / register / tempo carries the piece; the "timeline vs lead" polarity organizes the texture.
- **Meter parametricity via eighth-pulse beat-groups over a fine grid**, emitted in quarter-beats — additive meters fall out for free and the performer stays a linear beat→seconds map.
- **Deliberately generic folk** (a clave/bell timeline + Euclidean parts), not a named tradition, to avoid the exoticism trap (Agawu, `west-african-rhythm.md`). Flagged as an open question.
