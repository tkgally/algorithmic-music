**Scope:** Build Engine 04 — an engine inspired by small groups whose instruments are played with great per-note expressivity (string quartet, jazz sax, rock lead guitar): four original expressive voices, expression tied to the compositional structure, original expressivity sliders, an original genre. Autonomous; merge to main.

## What Tom asked

> Create one more engine (04) inspired by small groups that include instruments played with great expressivity in the realization of individual notes — string quartets, small-group jazz (sax), some rock (electric lead guitar). It should both create interesting, well-structured compositions and have at least one voice realized with great expression, realized not just note-by-note but in relation to the overall melodic and compositional structure. Create four distinct instruments with that expressivity; the choice and combination varies with seed and user selection. Type and degree of expressivity changeable with original sliders. Can also use less-expressive instruments (keyboards, rhythm guitar, bass, percussion). Don't imitate actual instrument sounds — create original voices sharing the expressive characteristics. Compositional structures and genre can be original. Do it autonomously and merge to main.

## The diagnosis that shaped the design

The instruments Tom named are not united by their *sound* but by what a player does **while a single note sounds**: a bowed string scoops and vibrates and swells; a sax bends, growls, and falls; an electric lead bends, sustains, and overdrives. That is all **continuous intra-note control** of pitch and amplitude. Every voice in the shared `synth.js` before this engine set its pitch **once** (`osc.frequency.setValueAtTime`) — perfect for struck/plucked/pad timbres, structurally incapable of these gestures. So the engine's foundation had to be a new class of voice, and its soul had to be tying that expression to *structure* (the [expressive-performance](../../wiki/expressive-performance.md) mandate — "expression clarifies musical structure"; the cure for "mechanical performance" is structured deviation, not randomness).

## What I built — `cantabile@0.1.0` (`docs/engines/04-cantabile/`)

An original genre — a small ensemble that **sings every note**. *Cantabile* = "in a singing style."

### 1. Continuous intra-note expression (the new synthesis layer, `experiments/lib/synth.js`, additive)

A new note contract for expressive voices: `note.expr` (all fields optional/defaulted) + `note.pan`, realized through three shared helpers:

- **`pitchExpr`** — automates each oscillator's `detune` param (cents, additive, so a baked-in `frequency` chorus offset is undisturbed): an **onset glide** (scoop from below / fall from above / legato **portamento** from the previous note's pitch), an optional **release bend** (sax fall / guitar release), and a **delayed, blooming vibrato** LFO summed into the same param (depth ramps in after a delay; rate lifts ~12% toward the note end, per Prame 1994). All ramps to defined endpoints → deterministic and browser-identical.
- **`exprEnv`** — an amplitude envelope with an intra-note **swell** (*messa di voce*): attack → rise into a peak partway through → ease to a sustain → release, all exponential ramps + a final linear ramp to **true zero** (anti-click). A held note *breathes*, which a fixed ADSR cannot.
- **`breathLayer`** (bow/breath grain) and **`panTo`** (per-voice `StereoPanner` — chamber seating).

### 2. Four original expressive voices (imitating no real instrument)

All from stock Web Audio nodes; all share the one expression surface, differ only in tone generation:
- **`aria`** — saw + triangle through a resonant lowpass whose cutoff opens toward the swell peak ("bow pressure"); the warm middle voice, biggest swell.
- **`reed`** — a sawtooth split into a resonant "formant" bandpass (nasal peak) + a flat body path; breath-forward; the reediest.
- **`wire`** — two detuned saws through a soft-clip **overdrive whose drive blooms toward the note peak** (grit grows as the player leans in) + a resonant lowpass; longest sustain, widest vibrato.
- **`glass`** — sine + two faint shimmered partials, a breath chiff; the purest, most crystalline (musical-saw/theremin in spirit).

Plus a new **`pluck`** support voice (soft plucked-harmonic comp/arpeggio) — the deliberate un-expressive foil, alongside the reused `bass`. Each expressive voice also carries an **expressive personality** in the performer (`TIMBRE` profiles: scoop/vibrato magnitudes, breath affinity, stereo seat), so the same slider settings read differently per voice.

### 3. Expression tied to structure — the composer (`experiments/composers/expressive-chamber.js`)

An original modal genre shaped as a **dramatic arc** so there is structure to express:
- Six lyrical modes (dorian, aeolian, lydian, mixolydian, phrygian, harmonic minor); **non-functional / plagal** harmony (no V–I — those are Engine 01's); a per-seed **germ motif** (rhythm + opening contour) that seeds most phrases (motivic economy).
- Seven sections — **intro · theme · dialogue · rise · climax · return · coda** — with a rising-then-falling **intensity** target. The **dialogue** trades two-bar phrases between lead and partner (call-and-response); the **rise** fragments/sequences the motif upward; the **coda** is a real modal close on a long, held, swelling **tonic**.
- Every note is stamped with a structural **weight (0..1)** — from metric position, length, phrase-arch position, apex/goal/climax membership. This is the composer↔performer interface.

### 4. The performer — expression driven by structure (`experiments/engines/cantabile/engine.js`)

`drive = Expression × weight × (0.5 + 0.7·Ardor·sectionIntensity)`, and every gesture (scoop/portamento depth, vibrato depth, swell, brightness, grit) scales by it. Result: measured over a render, **climax notes carry ~3–4× the vibrato depth and higher brightness of intro notes** (asserted by test); raising **Ardor** widens the gap. Plus a phrase-arch tempo (Rubato), a laid-back lead lag, a small correlated AR(1) residual, dynamic arch, and legato articulation.

### 5. Original expression sliders (the brief asked for original parameters)

Master **Expression** (KTH global-*k* analog) + **Song** (vocal pitch inflection) · **Bloom** (how a held note develops) · **Ardor** (dramatic intensification tied to the arc) · **Rubato** (timing breath) · **Grain** (air/texture). Each maps to a *distinct* mechanism, not a generic mix knob. Honest gating: at Expression = 0 the render has **no vibrato and no scoops** (played straight); a test asserts it.

### 6. Ensemble varies by seed + user selection

A deterministic per-seed shuffle assigns lead / partner (answering) / inner (sustained color) from the four voices; the user can override lead and partner (or choose a solo lead). Over 40 seeds every one of the four leads at least once (asserted). UI: two-level controls, editable seed, **full state in the URL hash** (ensemble + every slider), self-report (form/sections/intensity/motif/ensemble), a role-colored piano-roll, and a download-JSON feedback affordance.

## Key decisions

- **Additive-only, low-risk change.** Only `synth.js` changed (5 new voices + helpers, appended — no existing line touched). **`fx.js` untouched**; the expressive voices route through the existing **melody** bus (leads) and pan **in-voice**, `pluck` through the **chord** bus. So engines 01–03 keep their own vendored libs and are **byte-for-byte unaffected** (verified: their `synth.js` has none of the new voices; their render gates still 16/16). No re-vendor into other engines.
- **Pitch expression on additive `detune`, chorus offsets baked into `frequency`.** Keeps the expression math (a single cents signal per oscillator) clean and lets `pitchExpr` own `detune` entirely.
- **All automation to defined endpoints** (`linearRamp`/`exponentialRamp`, minimal `setTargetAtTime`) so the offline render is deterministic and matches every browser — the lesson from the Engine-01/02 static saga (a `setTargetAtTime`→ramp transition differs across browsers).
- **Structural weight as the composer↔performer interface** — composer owns "what matters," performer owns "how to show it," separately testable; guarantees expression is structural, not jitter.
