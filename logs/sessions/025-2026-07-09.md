## Result — cantabile → 0.2.0

Shared voices (`bass`/`rhodes`/`keys`/`strings`/`bell`/`pad`/`drone`/drums) and `fx.js` are untouched, and engines 01–03 keep their own older vendored `synth.js` (which has none of the expressive voices), so **they are byte-for-byte unaffected** — the regression render gates confirm it.

### (1) Varied, non-overwhelming accompaniment — the composer

The v0.1 bed was the flaw Tom named exactly: the comp was one `[0,1,2,3]` ascending arpeggio of equal 1-beat notes, and the bass a bare root on beat 1 — identical every bar. Rewrote the harmonic-bed section of `experiments/composers/expressive-chamber.js` around:

- **Per-seed comping templates** — `pad`, `padSoft`, `spacious`, `bassChord` ("boom-chick"), `bassChord2`, `broken`, `arpFlow` (mixed note lengths), `syncStab` — each a list of `[beatInBar, durBeats, selector]`. A template is chosen per bar from a **section-appropriate set** (`COMP_BY_SECTION`) and **never repeated two bars running**. This kills the equal-length/equal-shape monotony directly (comp onsets/bar went from a constant 1-or-4 to a 2–8 range with nine distinct note durations).
- **Inter-chord voice leading** (`voiceComp`) — each bar's voicing is the chord's pitch classes chosen **nearest to the previous bar's voicing**, so the comp is a smooth bed that moves by steps, not a line that jumps. (A test asserts the comp centroid moves < ~5 semitones/bar on average.) This is the long-carried "inter-chord voice leading" increment.
- **A varied bass** (`BASS_TEMPLATES` + `bassTone`) — root, root/fifth alternation, root-then-octave, a **walking** approach into the next chord, an **anticipation** pushing over the barline, and a sparse `rest1` that enters on beat 2. Section-appropriate (`BASS_BY_SECTION`): sparse in intro/coda and under the dialogue, walking in rise/climax. (Bug found and fixed along the way: `theory.scale()` runs *upward* from its root, so the original low-register pool filter came up empty and the walking-bass selector returned `undefined` → a `null` freq; fixed by building the low pool two octaves down, plus a finite-value guard in `bassTone`.)
- **Kept low and supportive** — the performer's `voiceBase` for comp (0.5) and the low comp weight keep comp velocity ≈ 0.32 against the leads' ≈ 0.65; density tracks section intensity; the bed sits below the lead register. It never covers the singing voices.

### (2) More organic, less-synthetic lead timbre — the synth

Diagnosed *why* an oscillator-plus-filter voice reads as "a synthesizer" and wrote it up as a standing wiki page — **`wiki/original-sound-design.md`** — because this is a project-wide issue, not just Engine 04's. The six causes: dead-steady pitch (the strongest tell), no fixed resonant body, static spectrum, a too-clean/undifferentiated attack, perfectly harmonic phase-locked partials, a perfectly periodic waveform. Added three of the page's techniques to the four expressive voices as **additive helpers in `synth.js`** (deterministic + browser-identical: incommensurate fixed-rate LFOs and band-limited slices of the shared seeded noise buffer, every ramp to a defined endpoint):

- **`microDrift`** — 3 incommensurate sub-Hz sine LFOs (per-voice rate sets) summed into `detune`, ~2–5 cents. **Always on** (a real instrument's pitch is alive even "played straight"), grain-forward via the Grain slider, and different rates per voice so simultaneous voices drift out of lockstep (they beat like a real section). This is the single biggest step from "patch" to "instrument."
- **`bodyResonance`** — a small stack of **fixed** `peaking` filters per voice (warm low-mid + presence peaks for `aria`, a nasal pair for `reed`, "cabinet" peaks for `wire`, one gentle peak for `glass`) that do **not** track the played pitch, with a level-compensating trim — the cue that a physical body is resonating the tone.
- **`onsetTransient`** — a short per-voice attack noise: bow `scratch` (aria), `breath` chiff (reed), pick `click` (wire); glass keeps its existing airy chiff. The attack carries most of the identification, so this both de-genericizes and separates the voices.

Performer sets a new `expr.drift` (cents), grain-forward with a small lift from expressiveness and a floor so it never fully disappears.

### (3) Vocal-like phrasing and breathing — composer + performer

- **Composer:** lead lines are now emitted as **breath-delimited, motif-seeded sub-phrases** via a new `leadPhrases` helper — a section's bars are split into 2–3-bar chunks (no orphan 1-bar tails), each realized by `makePhrase` (which already opens from the germ motif and liquidates onto a long resting goal tone), and each chunk's goal is tagged **`breath`**. So instead of one long undifferentiated line per section-half, the lead sings a phrase, arrives, and there is a place to breathe — every ~2 bars, like a singer. The intro's held note and each dialogue trade's final note are also tagged `breath`. (Tests: ≥5 breath points, all on melodic voices; still exactly one held `ending` tonic and it is not a breath.)
- **Performer:** realizes each breath as a real **silence** — it releases the phrase-final note a little early (a *lift*, scaled by Rubato and capped, not a clip); **blocks portamento across a breath** so the next phrase begins with a fresh onset; adds a small **agogic hesitation** entering a new phrase (a breath takes time); and eases the dynamics down into the breath. Within a phrase the articulation stays connected (legato). (A test asserts breath notes leave more silence than mid-phrase legato notes and an audibly positive gap.)

## Key decisions

- **Additive, engine-04-local, zero cross-engine risk.** New synth helpers + edits confined to the four expressive voice functions; the shared voices and `fx.js` are untouched. No re-vendor into engines 01–03 (only `docs/engines/04-cantabile/` re-vendored). The regression gates prove it.
- **Organic timbre is a *voice* property, not a *performance* one.** `microDrift` is always on (independent of the Expression slider) because a real instrument is alive even played straight — but the existing `Expression = 0 → no vibrato/scoops` contract is preserved (drift is neither vibrato nor scoop).
- **Reused `makePhrase` as the sub-phrase unit** for breathing rather than retrofitting breaths into a free walk — each `makePhrase` already opens from the motif and lands on a resting goal, so it *is* a sung phrase; tagging its goal `breath` gets vocal phrasing almost for free and keeps the existing tests valid.
- **Determinism preserved throughout** (seeded RNG streams for the templates; fixed-rate LFOs + seeded noise for the organic timbre) so the offline render gate stays stable and browser-identical.
