So the offline render *is* the thing to analyze, and the WAV independently confirmed which windows are rough.

## Diagnosis (offline, headless Chromium + OfflineAudioContext)

Built a probe harness (per-voice + reverb-on/off isolation renders; STFT spectrograms → PNG; a broadband/flatness detector; a **grain** energy-CV; a proper **roughness** metric; and a pure active-note lister). Findings, in order:

1. **Reverb is not the cause.** `full_rev` and `full_dry` have essentially identical roughness at every window (dry ≈ wet). Session 021's reverb-grain smoothing was real but is marginal *in the full mix* (the dry voices dominate). Hypothesis 1 ruled out.
2. **No clicks / envelope discontinuities.** `maxStep` ≈ 0.10 (the ordinary steep segments of legitimate waveforms), no hard step; the flagged "instants" are continuous beating (tremolo-like) in the waveform, not clicks. Hypothesis 2 (envelope corners) ruled out as the cause.
3. **A metric trap, then the right metric.** A 20 ms short-time energy **CV** *rose* when I tightened the pad detune — because that window averages away *fast* beating (roughness) and over-weights *slow* beating (chorus). Switching to an **amplitude-envelope modulation spectrum** (energy in **20–90 Hz** = perceived roughness/"static"; **1–10 Hz** = pleasant shimmer) fixed the confusion.
4. **Per-voice synth tweaks don't help.** Darker/tighter pad and softer/shorter bell did **not** lower the flagged-window roughness — because they don't reduce the *number* of colliding partials.
5. **Active-note analysis cracked it.** Listing the partials sounding at each flagged instant vs a clean control showed the flagged times are **structural**: near-coincident partial pairs jump from ~9 (clean single region) to 16–40 at the flagged times. Two triggers:
   - **Region crossfades** (01:59, and 00:42–45): the composer held the *departing* region's full pad voicing at near-full level for an **8 s** overlap while the *arriving* voicing was already full → **two complete modal voicings** beat within critical bands (the [auditory-perception-basics](../wiki/auditory-perception-basics.md) / Plomp & Levelt "a pad exposes tuning" mechanism).
   - **Dense bell clusters** (01:37–43, the roughest point in the real WAV; 02:09): the five incommensurable loops occasionally line up so **five long, inharmonic bells** (1 : 2.01 : 2.98 : 4.2) pile into a rough high cluster ("midrange and high notes").

**Root cause: sensory roughness from texture density, in the composer — not the synth or the reverb.**

## Fix (`experiments/composers/ambient-drift.js`; ambient-only)

- **True pad crossfade:** pad region overlap **8 s → 4 s** (`padOverlap`), so the old voicing has faded out (its envelope release) before the new is fully in — never two full voicings at once. The **drone keeps its 8 s overlap** (`droneOverlap`) — a smooth sub with no upper partials adds no roughness and keeps the bass continuous under the crossfade (no gap in the bed; the render gate's silence check still passes at 0.3–0.7 s).
- **Bell polyphony cap (≤ 4)** + mild ring trim (upper bound 9 s → 7.5 s): deterministic, earliest-onset-wins, the focal "arrival" tone always kept. Thins only the densest moments (~13 % of hits at the default seed); the loops still overlap and never realign, so the *Music for Airports* idea is intact.

**`synth.js`/`fx.js` are unchanged** (verified not the cause), so **tonal-classical and groove-lofi are untouched** — no re-vendor to them, no version bumps. Only **ambient-drift → 0.1.2** (composer + engine re-vendored into `docs/engines/02-ambient-drift/`).
