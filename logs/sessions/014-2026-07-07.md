**`generative-music-design-patterns.md`** — no issues found. All 24 links resolve; every prior-art/mechanism claim checked against its dependency pages held up, including the two explicit cross-references to failure-modes.md ("failure mode 10," "failure mode 12"). Same reasoning as above for why it survived unscathed. Promoted to `reviewed`.

**`engine-architecture.md`** — one real drift found and fixed, as anticipated. The "Runtime rules" section said engines use "vanilla ES modules or a single classic script." But the session-010 shared-library prototype (`wiki/findings-shared-lib-foundation.md`) established that cross-file ES-module `import` is CORS-blocked under `file://`, and that vendored libraries must be dual-format (UMD-lite) classic scripts loaded via `<script src>`. Rewrote the bullet: engines are either a single classic script or several classic-script files loaded via multiple `<script src>` tags (a vendored library plus the engine's own code) — no cross-file ES-module `import`/`export`. Added `shared-libraries.md` and `findings-shared-lib-foundation.md` to Related pages. Everything else on the page (RNG-stream design, lookahead scheduling, worklet `file://` caveats, loudness discipline, feedback-JSON-form status, the three Open Questions) held up unchanged and none of the open questions are newly resolved. Promoted to `reviewed`.

This is the kind of drift the doctrine-verification approach is specifically good at catching: a synthesis page's claim about its own project's *other* pages going stale as those pages evolve, distinct from the source-misattribution/wrong-number errors the earlier domain-page verify sweeps mostly found.
