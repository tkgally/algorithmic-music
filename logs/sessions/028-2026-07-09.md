## Tom's directive (primary source, preserved verbatim)

This arrived as the session prompt. It is the project's most important piece of direction since bootstrap; [comprehensive-site-vision](../../wiki/comprehensive-site-vision.md) distills it and cites this log as the source. Quoted in full:

> Read continue-prompt.md and orient yourself to this project. You have created 5 music-composition engines so far, and I am quite satisfied with the progress being made.
>
> I have decided on the long-term goal for this project: A self-contained website with no external dependencies that composes and performs instrumental music in a wide variety of styles and forms. Some of those styles and forms are based on pre-established ones described in previous research and discussions of music as reported in the wiki, while others are original, created on the fly by the website's code. The original styles and forms draw on high-order principles about piece structure, tonal quality, rhythm, harmony, melody, etc. that are inspired by previous genres of music while incorporating imaginative and unprecedented elements of their own. The ultimate goal is to create music that is pleasing, inspiring, and interesting to human listeners, so human psychology and response remain important elements. I will offer my personal feedback later on beta versions of the site, but you should incorporate what is known about human perception and response to music in general when designing and building the site.
>
> The interface of the website will be similar to that of the engines you have already created, except that there will be three levels of controls: the Start level, in which the user is confronted with a fairly small number of familiar genre categories and parameters, and the seed generation chooses from among those genres and parameters; the Intermediate level offers about 20 more detailed but still familiar controls; and the Advanced level a wide range of other controls, including many for parameters created originally for this website. The specifics of those controls will be suggested by you and reviewed by me through multiple sessions in the future.
>
> When first accessing the site, the user will choose from a few choices at the Start level and press Play. A random seed that includes those choices will be generated, and the piece will play. As with the current engines, the seed and settings will also appear as a suffix to the URL, and the same full URL will yield the same piece every time played. (The seed and other settings will be encoded in the URL suffix more compactly than at present; the details do not need be user-decipherable in the URL itself.) The seed can be copied to the clipboard, too. Other interface elements, such as playlists, will be developed later.
>
> This new comprehensive site will have a large set of shared JS libraries that you will create for it. The code in those JS files will be annotated clearly to make clear what each part of the code does or is for. The code will be created originally and entirely by you. You will not use any code from existing libraries. The entire site will be in one folder, with a well-organized set of subfolders containing scripts, etc.
>
> Before you begin building this new comprehensive site, I first want you to spend a full session expanding and enhancing the wiki. Because the wiki will be the key source of knowledge and information for this project, I want it to have very wide coverage of every aspect of music and related topics so that the design of the final website will be able to incorporate as much knowledge as possible about music as it has been produced by humans in all its variety. That variety will also serve as inspiration for the original styles of music created by the site.
>
> Spend this entire session working on the wiki. You (Fable 5) should serve as the overall supervisor, designer, and orchestrator of this wiki enhancement session. When assigning research and other tasks to subagents, use Sonnet 5 and/or Opus 4.8.
>
> If you have any questions before you begin, please ask me now.
