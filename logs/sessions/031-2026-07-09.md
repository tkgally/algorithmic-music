### 2. Live setting changes — designed and confirmed feasible (new §8)

**Confirmed: yes, feasible, and the better architecture.** The test engines restart only because each **composes the whole piece up front**; the site instead **composes just-in-time** — a bar/phrase ahead of the playhead, reading the *current* control values each step (extending the lookahead scheduler from scheduling to composing). A change then lands on not-yet-generated material and the piece morphs forward with no restart — the standard interactive-generative-music model (game-audio RTPCs, live-coding, Eno's apps). Three **response speeds** per control: **instant** (mix/sound — reverb, brightness, width, volume, expression: ramps in place), **next bar/phrase** (local-structural — density, mode, palette, swing, meter: musical, not mid-note), **re-plan from here** (global/form and genre/blend changes — remaining timeline recomputed from the current position, with a short transition for big jumps). **URL/determinism (Tom's concern):** the URL keeps mirroring the *current settings snapshot + seed*, so sharing reproduces the destination played from the top (compact, deterministic) — not the exact live knob-twiddling gesture; an optional Advanced "capture performance" (record the automation timeline) is noted and deferred. This is a first-class architectural commitment (flagged to fold into `engine-architecture.md` and the site-architecture doc; queue item P3-3 updated).
