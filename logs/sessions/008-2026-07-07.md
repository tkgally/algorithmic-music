## Decisions / assumptions

- Same discipline as session 007: treated quoted material as the highest-risk claim type and briefed every subagent to check exact wording against the actual cited source, not just plausibility. This is what caught all three west-african-rhythm misquotes and the east-asian guqin/Sujecheon overstatements — a looser "does this seem right" check would have passed all of them, since the underlying facts are directionally true.

- For the one quote that a subagent could only "softly" verify (Agawu's "a constant search for difference" in the JAMS 48/3 article — citation and substance confirmed, but the exact standalone phrase couldn't be independently pulled from full text), left it as-is: the citation details are solid and the framing matches the article's argument, so this doesn't rise to the same bar as the three confirmed misquotes above.
