**counterpoint-and-voice-leading.md** — held up almost entirely. The subagent extracted Huron (2001)'s actual PDF text and confirmed, verbatim, both the "principal purpose of voice-leading" quote and the exact six perceptual principles (toneness, temporal continuity, minimum masking, tonal fusion, pitch proximity, pitch co-modulation) — a genuinely reassuring result given how much of the page rests on this one source. Re-sourced the claim to Bregman's auditory-scene-analysis account (as discussed within Huron 2001 itself), removed the Gray & Bunescu citation, added a Bregman 1990 source entry.

**rhythm-and-meter.md** — three fixes, one arithmetic:
- Moelants (2002)'s preferred-tempo claim was backwards in emphasis: the wiki said "centered around 100–120," but the paper's actual contribution is arguing *against* the older ~100 BPM estimate and revising the center *upward* to ~120–130. Corrected.
- "Pulse reversal" was the wiki's own gloss, not Fitch & Rosenfeld (2007)'s term — their paper (fetched in full) uses "resetting" throughout, one of three named strategies (coexistence, assimilation, resetting), with a reported r=.78 correlation to the syncopation index. Replaced the term everywhere it appeared (body text, implications, open questions) and cited the correlation.
- A derived-quantity slip: the page said IOI bounds of "~100–200 ms" imply a tactus rate of "~5–6 events/s," but 100 ms = 10 events/s and 200 ms = 5 events/s, so the correct derived range is ~5–10 events/s. Fixed.
- Everything else — the GTTM four metrical well-formedness rules, the LHL weight/syncopation model (confirmed exactly against Fitch & Rosenfeld's own restatement), Witek et al.'s polyphonic extension, Friberg & Sundström's swing-ratio numbers, all five of Toussaint's named Euclidean examples, and the E(5,16)-vs-son-clave distinction — checked out exactly against primary or high-quality secondary sources (Wikipedia's own Euclidean-rhythm and clave pattern tables corroborated the son-clave/E(5,16) distinction precisely).

**groove-and-embodiment.md** — the most substantive fixes of the five pages, on the domain's single most load-bearing empirical result:
- Witek et al. (2014)'s central numbers (66 participants, 50 funk drum-breaks, 120 BPM, R²≈.35 movement/≈.43 pleasure) were confirmed **exactly** against the fetched full paper (.3474 and .4267 precisely).
- "Senn et al. 2022" for the compact reusable stimulus set was a **wrong author attribution** — the actual paper is Stupacher, Wrede & Vuust (2022), PLOS ONE. Corrected the citation.
- More importantly, the subagent surfaced a finding not previously in the wiki: a 2024/25 study from Senn's own Lucerne group — with Maria Witek herself as a co-author — used a new 40-pattern stimulus set and found **no significant effect of complexity on urge-to-move** (p=.834), suggesting the original inverted-U may partly reflect the experimenter-composed (vs. idiomatic, drummer-composed) nature of the original stimuli. Added this as a caveat in the body and a new open question — the page now presents the inverted-U as the best current default rather than a settled law.
- Dropped an unsupported "brainstem" claim from the Hove et al. (2014, PNAS) bass-timing-perception finding; their own model supports only a cochlear (peripheral) mechanism.
- The low-frequency/RMS groove-predictor claim had no source in the page's bibliography at all — added Stupacher, Hove & Janata (2016), the actual paper behind that specific finding.
- Softened the ghost-note velocity numbers (≈10–30, ±5–10) from stated-as-sourced to an explicit informed estimate: the cited practitioner articles agree ghost notes sit "well below ~70" but give worked examples ranging from the 20s to the high 70s, and none states the exact ±5–10 repeated-hit figure.
- Swapped a RITMO citation that was actually about body posture (not the sound-feature claim it was attached to) for an accurate description, noting the sound-feature claim (duration/intensity/centroid shaping timing feels) is better supported by a companion paper this pass didn't pin down exactly — flagged for a future session.
