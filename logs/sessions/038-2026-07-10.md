## The design, and why (the original-reading part)

### 1. A style is a generative system, so invention must generate a system

Read together, the wiki's design pillar makes one specific claim: [style-and-genre-overview] models a style as a probabilistic constraint system; [style-invention-and-style-space] defines invention as placing a small coherent *region* (novelty budget over universals, 2–3 signatures, teach-the-style-inside-the-piece); [meta-composition-and-style-machines] dissects every successful style machine into *alphabet + selection procedure + control surface*. The eight genre packs are exactly such systems — hand-written. So the dedicated composer could not be smarter routing or wider parameter ranges. It has to **draw a small generative system per seed** (the *kernel*) and provide **one realizer that can perform any kernel**. The kernel is Tom's "freshly generated engine"; `invent.js` is its interpreter.

### 2. The kernel's axes are architectural, not scalar

The decisive influence was [texture-and-density]: the biggest audible differences between human musics are texture *architectures* — how the ensemble relates — not slider values. So the kernel's first draw is one of **eight texture architectures**, each an explicit writer:

| texture | mechanism (source) |
|---|---|
| melodyAccomp | tune over comping templates + bass (the homophonic default) |
| ostinatoWeb | fixed bass ground + mid ostinato on a *different* cycle length (2–3 bars) re-phasing against it + free lead (passacaglia/minimalism) |
| callResponse | lead call, then the mids answer with the motif's rhythm in chords — or the drums answer (antiphony) |
| strata | slow foundation, half-note skeleton converging on pillar goal tones, 4×/8× lead figuration around it, colotomic low punctuation (gamelan) |
| canon | the lead echoed at 1–2 beats' delay, transposed down a diatonic 4th/5th/octave, the echo filling the lead's phrase-end breath (imitation = cheap audible coherence) |
| hocket | one composite line split into on-beat and off-beat halves between lead and mid, converging on shared goal tones; below intensity 0.45 the lead alone carries a thinner composite, so the interlock IS the thickening (kotekan) |
| chorale | homorhythm: one shared phrase rhythm, all pitched voices move together, breath at phrase ends |
| tintinnabuli | M-voice in slow steps + T-voice locked to tonic-triad tones by the kernel's fixed position/side rule (Pärt's two-voice algorithm — the wiki's cleanest historical case of a *successful invented style*) |

Weights 0.20/0.17/0.14/0.12/0.12/0.10/0.08/0.07 keep the safest common. This is deliberately NOT the "one giant probability soup" anti-pattern: one architecture is drawn and committed; each is a legible, named system.

The other kernel axes: a **rhythm system** (flow · one Schillinger resultant cell stamped everywhere · a timeline (clave/bell/Euclid/sieve) · a kit groove), a **melodic gamut** (a seeded 5–7-degree subset of the scale — an invented mode-within-the-mode whose gaps are audible identity, while harmony keeps the full scale, the way pentatonic melodies ride parent harmony; tonic + signature-cell degrees always kept), **hierarchy pillars** (two degrees made frequent, long, and goal — the Castellano teach-the-hierarchy-by-distribution move), per-texture parameters, and an **entry mode** (layered/leadFirst/together).

### 3. Anchors, novelty, and coherence

Everything *not* drawn stays at [musical-universals] anchors (isochronous pulse, discrete pitches, stable tonic — the drone/bass holds the reference; arched contours, motivic repetition, ≤7-tone scales). The existing novelty budget (1–3 axes) and signature rules are kept and now *bind tighter*: a rhythm-motto signature becomes the theme's actual motif; the interval-cell biases the gamut; the cadenceDrop signature becomes the composed ending's melodic fall; voicing habits and echo tails were already realized centrally. New **coherence couplings** repair (never resample) kernel draws: tintinnabuli → drone/modal harmony + low density (the T-voice is triad-locked); chorale → density/tempo caps (homorhythm at 160 BPM is machine-gun chords) + richer voicings; hocket → BPM/density floors; strata → slower harmonic rhythm under the dense surface; ostinatoWeb → functional harmony becomes a loop (a ground wants a cycle); plus a general **chord-rate ceiling** (a chord change every <0.6 s reads as texture, not harmony — caught live by a symbolic audit, see below).

### 4. Form as grammar with guaranteed return and late peak

Sections are generated from a functional grammar (intro? · state · return · depart · break? · peak · end), flavored by the development value (cyclic/arch/through). Non-negotiables from [form-and-structure] + [tension-and-release] + the failure-modes checklist: **every form contains at least one recognizable return** (even through-composed recapitulates once); the **peak lands in the second half** at forced high intensity; endings are **composed idioms** (cadence = broadening + gamut descent onto the tonic; fade = layered thinning; stop = one unison hit; ringout = held tonic + bell/gong). The theme (motif rhythm + contour) is committed at the first statement and kept invariant across returns — the repetition-page's "recognizable invariant, vary around it." Length fitting **extends the form instead of inflating sections**: blocks cap at 16 bars; past the cap the grammar inserts another departure–return cycle before the peak, so long pieces become rondos rather than endless middles.

### 5. Full-ensemble voicing (the headline fix)

Every drawn role classifies to a writer (lead / harmonic mid / color mid / low / percussion), with graceful promotion when roles are missing (Advanced checkboxes can produce odd ensembles). The **percussion writer runs for every kernel**: inviolable timeline, kick with beat 1 sacred, snare backbeat + ghost notes, hat/shaker subdivision with a velocity hierarchy, two hand-percussion parts on **complementary Euclidean rotations** (the second rotation is search-chosen to minimize overlap with the first — genuine interlock), colotomic boom every two bars, gong at section starts. Activity is gated by the intensity arc per role (the layering is the dynamic form — fixes FM7 uniform density), and a per-texture quietness factor scales *velocity, never existence* — a chorale's drawn percussion is discreet, not deleted. Tested: 12 seeds × every drawn role emits ≥1 event.

### 6. Integration decisions

- **Not a registry pack.** Pack `order` doubles as the URL genre enum and the Start-button list; the invented strategy plugs in beside the registry (`compose._setInvented`), so serialize/UI/registry are untouched.
- **Kernel on the vector; realized patterns in the plan.** Control changes and re-plans rebuild the vector from the same seed → the idiom survives live changes; patterns (timeline, interlock rotations, ostinati, theme) re-derive deterministically in `init`.
- **Old invented links change character** (the routing they relied on no longer exists); the same seed still reproduces the same *new* piece exactly. Presets/melds byte-identical in behavior (their sweeps + all prior tests unchanged).

## What the gates caught during the build

1. **Breaks must keep a pulse.** First break-writer version went near-silent for styles with no timeline → a 7.3 s symbolic gap (seed `17157075`, 7/8 at 90 BPM). Fix: the low sustains through the break; a tapering pulse holds the grid.
2. **Length fitting must extend form, not rooms.** 2/4 at ~150 BPM made 200+-bar targets balloon single sections to 40–64 bars; fixed with the section cap + insert-a-cycle rule.
3. **Chord-rate ceiling.** The `harmonicRhythm` novelty (up to 4/bar) × a 2.5-beat bar × 123 BPM = a chord every ~0.3 s (20 events/s, seed `9e37b`); the new coupling halved that seed's event rate.
