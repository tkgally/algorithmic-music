## What I did

### Engine 01 → v0.2 (the feedback pass)

- **The "static" was the reverb, not clipping.** The render peak was ≈ 0.58 (far below full scale), so it was never master overload. Diagnosis: the convolution reverb used a *bright, noisy* impulse response, so sustained chords — worst on the long, exposed final note with nothing after to mask it — produced a periodic high-frequency "fizz" wash re-excited on each chord change; and the master glue compressor (ratio 3 @ −20 dB) pumped that tail *up* as notes decayed. Fixes:
  - `fx.js`: darker, smoother IR (two cascaded one-pole lowpasses over the noise, more damping, a 4 ms fade-in), a **band-limited reverb return** (highpass 180 Hz + lowpass ~4.2 kHz), gentler glue (ratio 2 @ −12 dB), and less pad reverb send. Made the reverb **parameterizable** (`reverbSeconds`/`reverbDamp`/`returnLowpassHz`/`returnGain`/`chordSendScale`) so engines can pick their space.
  - `synth.js`: every envelope now ramps to **true zero** (a short linear tail after the exponential release) before its oscillators stop — removing stop clicks — and the pad's filter now **darkens** as it rings rather than brightening (less wash).
  - Verified with an ad-hoc tail probe: the final seconds decay **monotonically (0.0 dB post-peak swell)** and **dark** (HF first-difference ratio ≈ 0.00).
- **The mechanical feel was the per-note white jitter** — precisely the "drunk-drummer" tell `wiki/expressive-performance.md` warns against. Rebuilt the performer (`engine.js` `renderPlan`) around that page's "minimum viable humanization": a **nested phrase-arch tempo** (each 4-bar phrase's edges slower than its middle) over a **square-root final ritardando**; a coupled **dynamic arch** (swell to mid-phrase, ease at the cadence), **high-loud**, softened metric accents, melody emphasis; **structured articulation** (détaché lead, separated repeated notes, ringing cadence/long notes); a subtle **chord roll** (triad spread low→high); delayed **vibrato** on held melody notes (in `synth.js`); and a small **correlated (AR(1)) residual** on timing/dynamics (lag-1 autocorrelation ≈ 0.8, σ ≈ 3 ms, below the ~6 ms JND) instead of i.i.d. noise.
- **Tempo**: default **92 → 110 BPM**, slider range widened to 66–140.
- Bumped to `tonal-classical@0.2.0`; updated the engine README, the UI default, and the findings page.

### Engine 02 — ambient-drift (the contrast)

Chose **generative ambient** as the maximal contrast to metric, cadential tonal-classical while reusing the audible layer — and the roadmap's top-listed shortlist option. Built it from the single most-recommended recipe in `wiki/ambient-and-generative-genre.md`: **Eno's *Music for Airports* incommensurable-loop pattern** (sparse events on mutually-prime-ish periods that never realign) over a **slow modal drift** (the "drone plus one slowly moving element" the page calls a complete texture).

- `experiments/composers/ambient-drift.js`: a **seconds-based** score (ambient is time-based, not beat-based — an honest schema deviation). A drone+pad bed drifts through consonant modal regions (crossfaded, no cadence); five bell loops run on periods **17.5 / 23 / 28.5 / 32.1 / 40 s**, chosen so every pairwise ratio stays ≥ 0.045 from any small-integer ratio and the earliest near-realignment is beyond ~190 s. Four palettes (lydian / major-pentatonic / dorian / natural-minor).
- `experiments/engines/ambient-drift/engine.js`: a deliberately minimal performer — pace scaling, a small correlated residual, and a global fade in/out so the piece emerges from and dissolves into silence. No meter, no ritardando, full sustains. Long, dark reverb.
- Extended the shared `synth.js` with **`bell`** (modal decaying sines), **`pad`** (slow-attack, cutoff-LFO motion), **`drone`** (deep sub + fifth) — all documented recipes, click-safe.
- `docs/engines/02-ambient-drift/`: self-contained UI (palette / drift / space controls, a texture visualization, URL-hash sharing, self-report, JSON feedback). Added the Engine 02 card to the hub `docs/index.html` (and renumbered the "coming soon" card to Engine 03).

## Decisions / notes

- **Interpreted the tempo feedback as "raise the default by ~10–20 BPM"** (the JSON showed the listener had already pushed it to 118). 110 is a +18 compromise from 92.
- **Engine 02 chose ambient over lo-fi/groove.** Ambient reuses the most infrastructure, is the strongest *formal* contrast (meter/cadence/tracked-melody vs. none of them), and — importantly given the feedback was about audio cleanliness — carries the least click/percussion risk. A groove engine (needs drum voices + swing) is a good future Engine 03.
- **Kept both engines' shared code canonical in `experiments/` and vendored into `docs/`** (verified byte-identical). The `fx` reverb parameterization and the three new `synth` voices are genuine shared-library growth, not genre-coupled hacks.
