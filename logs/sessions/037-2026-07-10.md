# Session 037 — 2026-07-10 — Invented-style ensembles (6→32) + name collisions

**Scope.** Tom asked (after a Q&A about how "Invent a style" works) for two focused refinements to invented styles, plus a hand-off prompt for Fable to build a **dedicated invented-style composer** in a future session. This session does the two refinements; the composer prompt is delivered separately to Tom (also preserved at the end of this log).

## What changed (both in `docs/lib/style.js`, `invent()`)

1. **A much larger, register-balanced ensemble template set (request 1).** The invented-style instrument templates went from **6 to 32**. Motivation: with only six templates, different invented seeds converged on similar instrumentation even when other features differed. Each template keeps a **register balance** — a HIGH `lead`, a MID `comp`/`pad`, and a LOW `bass`/`drone` — and **about half (16/32) add percussion** (a kit `kick`/`snare`/`hat`, or hand/world `perc1`/`perc2`). The templates moved to module level (a single shared const) and their registers/levels now reuse the `ROLE_REG`/`ROLE_LVL` tables added in session 036 (DRY). Over an 80-seed sample the number of distinct ensembles rose well past the old ceiling.

2. **Name collisions reduced (request 2).** Invented names went from `"Invented " + word + " " + number(1–98)` (8×99 = 792 possible labels) to `"Invented " + word + " " + lowercase-letter + 3-digit-number` — e.g. **"Invented Nocturne c402"** — for 8×26×1000 ≈ **208,000 labels** (~260× fewer accidental collisions). Matches Tom's requested format exactly. The name stays cosmetic and seed-derived (it does not describe the music); the **seed** remains the true unique identifier.

## Notes / honest limitations

- **`r.pick` makes one draw regardless of template-set size,** so the count of downstream RNG draws (novelty axes, signatures, name) is unchanged — only the *chosen* template differs. This means the change **does alter what a given invented seed produces** (it now picks from the larger pool); **presets and melds are unaffected** (they don't use these templates).
- **Percussion (and any melodic role beyond the routed strategy's small vocabulary) only *sounds* when the invented style routes to a strategy that voices those roles.** The eight genre strategies resolve parts with `ens.find(role)` over `lead`/`comp`/`bass` (+ fallbacks) and, for groove strategies, drum roles; percussion sounds on electronic/lofi/percussion routings. So the expanded templates reliably add audible variety in the **lead/comp/bass voices** (voiced everywhere), while the **percussion half sounds on groove routings**. Full-ensemble voicing for every invented piece is exactly what the planned **dedicated invented-style composer (request 3)** will provide — it should voice the whole drawn ensemble rather than borrow one of the eight genre engines. Left routing untouched this session per Tom's "do just (1) and (2)".
