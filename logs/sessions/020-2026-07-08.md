## What I built

### Engine 03 — `groove-lofi@0.1.0` (`docs/engines/03-groove-lofi/`)

The trio's groove member and the project's **first engine with drums** — a looped lo-fi hip-hop beat. It is a direct realization of `wiki/groove-and-embodiment.md`, whose central correction to this project's past work is that **groove comes from structured pattern + a velocity hierarchy, not timing noise** ("uniform random jitter is the worst option the literature identifies").

**Composer** (`experiments/composers/groove-lofi.js`, beats-based Note schema, pure/seeded):
- Four **moods**: mellow (major 7ths, ii–V–I–vi), night (natural-minor 9ths, i–iv–VI–v), warm (dorian, i–IV–i–v), tape (major, I–vi–ii–V). Seventh/ninth chord tones come from `theory` by tertian stacking (quality falls out of the mode).
- **Drums**: backbeat snare on beats 2 & 4 (the loudest recurring accent), a **medium-syncopation** kick from a bank that always keeps beat 1 (the meter anchor / Witek inverted-U peak), an eighth-note hat ride with loud/soft accents (+ occasional open hat), **ghost snares** (≈0.22) that enter in the B half, and one-bar snare fills at section hand-offs.
- **Bass**: root per chord in the low register + one syncopated movement note in the main groove (weight + a little motion).
- **Form**: intro (2 bars) → A → richer B (ghosts + a sparse bell lead + fills) → thinned outro. Length 16–40 bars.

**Performer** (`experiments/engines/groove-lofi/engine.js`, pure `renderPlan`): this is where groove actually happens, and deliberately **not** uniform jitter —
- **Swing** as a per-beat **phase warp** (remaps local phase so the eighth offbeat lands at `sw`, pushing both 8th and 16th offbeats late from one knob; downbeats never move). 0.5 = straight … ~0.66 heavy lo-fi.
- **Laid-back** microtiming: a small **fixed per-instrument** offset in seconds — snare drags ~18 ms behind, hats a hair, kick/bass tight — applied identically every cycle.
- A **velocity hierarchy** (from the composer) + a tiny hat swing-accent + a small **correlated (AR(1))** residual so cycles breathe without smearing.
- A **vinyl-crackle bed** (band-limited looped noise ≈ −40 dB × amount) added in the graph — deterministic, dial-able, and it renders in the offline gate.

**Shared-library additions** (`experiments/lib/synth.js` + `fx.js`, re-vendored into all three engines):
- Voices: `kick` (round sine pitch-drop), `snare` (tone + band-passed noise, velocity scales the noise so ghosts read as brushes), `hat` (high-passed noise, open/closed), `rhodes` (warm dusty 2-op FM EP). Drums share one **deterministic cached noise buffer** per context (offset slices per hit) per `synthesis-recipes.md`.
- Buses: kick full-range and **dry** (its weight is the groove), snare/hat high-passed + lightly sent, rhodes warmly sent; a small warm room (≈1.8 s) rather than the ambient engine's long tail.

**UI** (`index.html`): the same conventions as engines 01/02 — two-level controls (Mood, Tempo, Swing, Space, Vinyl; Advanced: Length, Key center, Seed), full state in the URL hash (shareable), a self-report table (progression roman numerals, backbeat, kick A/B patterns, ghosts/lead), a piano-roll visualization with drum lanes + a playhead, and a download-JSON feedback affordance.

## Decisions worth remembering

- **Groove is a performer phenomenon over a quantized grid.** The composer emits a clean beat grid; swing (phase warp) and the laid-back backbeat (fixed per-instrument offset) are added at performance time. This is the concrete answer to `groove-and-embodiment.md`'s "structured-not-random microtiming" and keeps the score honest/measurable.
- **The percussion voices live in the shared library, not the engine folder**, so future groove/electronic engines reuse them — same first-party-library model as the tonal/ambient voices.
- **The kick is dry and full-range**; sending it to the room would sap the low-frequency energy the groove research says drives movement.
- **The render gate's `maxStep` is per-engine**: sustained engines get a tight bound; the percussive engine gets a looser one because designed shaped-noise transients legitimately step more (this is explicitly allowed by `synthesis-recipes.md`).
